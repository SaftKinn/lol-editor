# ADR 0003 — English-facing channel, music-first (commentary later)

- Status: accepted
- Date: 2026-06-24

## Context
The owner's goal is growth + monetization, and asked which language/voice strategy gives the best
shot at success. Research findings: English-speaking audiences carry the highest CPM (often 5–10×
small-language markets) and far larger reach; gaming CPM is modest overall ($5–10 CPM), so
monetization needs reach + volume; Shorts RPM is tiny (Shorts = growth, long-form = revenue);
music-only highlight channels work, while commentary builds connection and suits funny/long-form.

## Decision
Make the channel **English-facing** — titles, on-screen text/overlays, and metadata in English —
for maximum reach and CPM. Start **music-first** (no voice), so videos ship fast and build the
funnel, since the content (highlights, Darius outplays, ARAM chaos) reads globally without speech.
Add **English commentary as a later layer** for long-form, where personality/retention pays off.

## Alternatives considered
- **German-facing:** closer to the owner, less competition, but a much lower reach/CPM ceiling —
  rejected against a monetization goal.
- **English commentary from day one:** stronger connection, but forces the owner on-mic
  immediately and slows shipping — deferred to a later layer.
- **Music-only forever:** simplest, but weak for the "funny/personality" angle and long-form
  retention — kept as the start, not the end state.

## Consequences
- Largest addressable audience and best ad economics for the effort.
- Fast initial production (no recording/scripting voice).
- Long-form will later need a commentary workflow (mic, light editing) — a future addition.
- All audience-facing text is authored in English (the metadata stage targets English).
